Eclipse plot, years 2000–2029

This plot shows every eclipse from the year 2000 through 2029. Solar eclipses are shown as orange dots (many) and lunar eclipses as grey dots (many). The horizontal axis is the year and the vertical axis is the month, so the diagonal bands of dots reveal the “eclipse seasons” that recur about twice a year. Move the pointer over a dot — or press Tab to step through the eclipses in date order — to read its date, type, Saros series, duration, and where it is visible. A full list is also given in the eclipse list below.

Key

Solar eclipse (orange dot) — types: Total, Annular, Hybrid, Partial.

Lunar eclipse (grey dot) — types: Total, Penumbral, Partial.

Shaded vertical bands mark the years 2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020 and 2025.
